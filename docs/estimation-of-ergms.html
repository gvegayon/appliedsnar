5.1 Estimation of ERGMs

The algorithm goes like this:

  1. Give an initial guess for \(\theta^{(t)}\), and simulate networks with that

  2. With the networks simulated, we can compute the gradient and hessian of the log-likelihood, and hence update the parameter \(\theta^{(t)}\to\theta^{(t+1)}\)

  3. If convergence has been reach (depending on the criteria) stop, otherwise go to step 1.

For more details see (Snijders 2002), and (Wang et al. 2009) provides a nice comparison between the algorithm used in ergm and in PNet (which is the same as the one used in RSiena).

References

Snijders, Tom AB. 2002. “Markov Chain Monte Carlo Estimation of Exponential Random Graph Models.” Journal of Social Structure 3.

Wang, Peng, Ken Sharpe, Garry L. Robins, and Philippa E. Pattison. 2009. “Exponential Random Graph (P*) Models for Affiliation Networks.” Social Networks 31 (1): 12–25. doi:https://doi.org/10.1016/j.socnet.2008.08.002.
